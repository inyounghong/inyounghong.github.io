                I am an <i id="artist" style="color:#ea3f53;">artist</i> .

                <div class="norm" style="font-size:24px"><br>
                <p>January 4th, 2012 was the day that I first stumbled across <a href="http://www.deviantart.com">DeviantART.com</a>, the world’s largest art website, and it was on that cold, unassuming Saturday that <em>my life as I know it now began</em>.</p>

                <p>It was by sheer chance that I happened to come across <a href="http://yuumei.deviantart.com/gallery/">yuumei</a>'s incredible gallery, but it was those long hours spent in awe over the beauty of her digital artworks that inspired me to try <em>digital art</em>, a medium I had never even known about before.</p>

                <p>As I explored deeper and deeper into digital art, I found myself <em>falling in love</em> with this medium that called to me as traditional art never had before. I adored the clarity of its brushstrokes, the vivacity of its colors, and the versatility of its infinite canvas which made me feel as though I could create <em>anything that I desired</em>.</p>

                <div class="norm" style="font-size:24px"><br>
                    <p>But my journey did not end there. After about a year of exploring digital art, I stumbled across another fascinating discovery: <u>web design</u>.</p>

                    <p>The moment I laid my eyes upon the web design languages of <u>HTML</u> and <u>CSS</u>, I was completely hooked. I threw myself into trying to crack the languages that made up the web, pouring over the codes written by other web designers and tinkering with each line one by one until I could feel my understanding beginning to grow. I published my first one-page web design in October of 2013, and since then I have continued to develop and fine-tune my skills by creating themes and skins for websites such as <u>DeviantART</u> and <u>Tumblr</u>. </p>
